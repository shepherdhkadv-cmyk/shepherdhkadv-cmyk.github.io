As a public sector client, we have considerable leverage with our contractors to instill a safety culture throughout the entire procurement process. This approach encompasses: selection of tenders, stipulation of contract requirements, monitoring contractors’ performance, motivating those who perform well and sanctioning those who fail to fulfill their obligations. Contractors, as employers, must then effectively cascade the safety culture through the supply chain right down to the frontline workers on site.

List Management and Procurement Systems

Housing Authority (HA) selects competent contractors through list management. List management is to assess the qualifications and standard of performance of contractors which are compliance with ISO9001, ISO14001, ISO45001 and ISO50001 and adopting policies for ensuring safe and healthy working condition.
HA takes responsive actions for unsatisfactory safety performance, occurrence of serious accident or near miss incident with potentially serious consequence

Collaborative Learning

Safety Training Programmes have been provided for resident site supervisory staff, professional staff and resident site management staff. By integrating these programmes which fosters a culture where knowledge is shared across roles, teamwork is strengthened, and collective responsibility for safety is emphasized. Interactive and web-based elements further support engagement and empower staff at all levels to contribute to a safer working environment.

Fatal and Accident Rate

On-going safety targets zero fatal accident and accident rate not more than eight accidents per thousand workers from 1 April 2023 onwards.

Contract Specification

Contract conditions require the Contractor to comply with the safety requirements including full responsibility for the adequate stability and safety of all operations on site and keep the site and the works in an orderly state appropriate to avoid danger to all persons.
Recent specification requirements in relation to safety cover use of metal scaffolding, tower crane, mobile crane etc., protection of works from heat stroke, provision of safety equipment and clothing for personal protection, 4S requirements etc. Temporary works requirements as per our specification is enforced to all contracts for the management system with approval process to the whole temporary works for safety.
Technical proposal of tender submission shall include capability to provide site specific proposals and precautionary measures to contain risks.
Control of temporary works, implementation of 4S and innovative technologies, i.e. construction robotics include in the specification and the Integrated Pay for Safety, Environment and Hygiene Scheme (IPSEHS). IPSEHS provides incentive whereby contractors are paid only if they have satisfactorily performed their obligations.

Housing Department Site Safety Sub-committee

To foster partnership for a safety culture, we have established the Housing Department Site Safety Subcommittee (HDSSSC) have been established as a platform to engage stakeholders in the review, appraisal and alignment of safety measures for works contracts.

Site Safety Forum

The Housing Authority collaborated with the Occupational Safety and Health Council and all external members of HDSSSC to organise Site Safety Forums as a corporate event to raise safety awareness and promote a safety culture.
We endeavor to encourage new and innovative ways to draw stakeholder engagement through interactive and lively discussion platforms on thematic safety issues.
