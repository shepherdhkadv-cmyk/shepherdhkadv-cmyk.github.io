Architectural Safety

The major hazardous areas for operation and maintenance in a building typically include the canopy, roof, re-entrant spaces, covered walkways on the building’s exterior, and the lift pit inside the building. To ensure safe operation and maintenance in these high-risk locations, appropriate safety measures are incorporated into the design, such as maintenance stairs, protective railings, gondolas, and maintenance platforms. These provisions facilitate safe access for maintenance workers and help reduce the risk of accidents. In addition to safe access for maintenance personnel, operational needs including material delivery and equipment handling should also be taken into consideration. Furthermore, a Maintenance and Repair (M&R) Access Plan must be prepared and submitted for approval prior to applying for consent to commence superstructure works. This plan ensures that all necessary access arrangements and safety features are in place to support ongoing maintenance activities safely and efficiently throughout the building’s lifecycle.

Safe Access to Lift Pit

Lifts require regular maintenance and inspections to ensure safe and reliable operation. At the lowest landing floor of every lift shaft, permanent anchorage points are installed to provide secure attachment for fall arrest systems and safety harnesses. These anchorage points are essential for protecting maintenance personnel when accessing the lift pit, significantly reducing the risk of falls and ensuring a safer working environment.
Furthermore, emergency stop switch inside lift shaft near the lift landing door of the lowest landing floor is also provided for the maintenance workers to stop the lift in order to avoid dangerous condition while working inside lift pit.

  • Anchorage points
  • Anchorage points

In addition, when the depth of a lift pit exceeds 2m, a staircase must be provided to ensure safe and convenient access for maintenance personnel. To prevent flooding in the lift pit, sump pits equipped with sump pumps and surface drainage channels are also installed. These safety measures help maintain a secure working environment by facilitating safe entry and exit, as well as protecting workers from hazards associated with water accumulation.

Protection for Work at Height

Protection against falls is one of the primary hazards prevention during maintenance and repair work on building façades and rooftops. To safeguard workers and prevent accidents, guardrails are installed along the edges of rooftops, flat roofs, and covered walkways. These protective barriers play a crucial role in creating a safe working environment by minimising the risk of falls from height.
In addition, essential provisions for the future installation of gondolas have been incorporated into the building design to facilitate safe maintenance of the façade. These include ensuring adequate structural strength for securely fixing clamps, providing facilities for tie-back anchors and safety ropes, and supplying electrical power to operate the gondola. These measures are critical to protecting maintenance workers by enabling safe and reliable access to the building exterior.

Air Conditioner Hood and Safe Maintenance Access

Scaffolding work is often required when replacing or maintaining air conditioning (A/C) units in domestic flats, which poses significant risks of falls from height. To enhance worker safety and reduce these risks, A/C hood have been introduced for all domestic flat units, allowing installation and maintenance work to be performed safely from inside the flat. Additionally, the size of A/C windows has been enlarged to accommodate a wider range of A/C units and facilitate safer access. To further minimise risks associated with cleaning and repairing condensation pipes from the exterior, these pipes are designed to be positioned close to openable windows, enabling easier and safer access for maintenance personnel. These design considerations collectively improve safety by reducing the need for external scaffolding and providing safer, more convenient access points for workers.

Building Services Safety

Lighting Control for Enhances Accessibility

Standard lighting illumination levels are provided throughout the public areas of domestic blocks to ensure general visibility and safety. To further enhance safety for residents and users with visual impairments, an adjustable lighting control system is installed. This system allows individuals to customise the lighting levels according to their needs, helping to reduce the risk of falls and improve overall accessibility and comfort within the building.

* The elevated illumination level will be automatically resumed to normal illumination level after an adjustable time delay.

Audible Lift Signals for Visually Impaired Users

Users with visual impairments often face challenges when using lifts, particularly in knowing when the lift has arrived. To address this, audible signals and verbal announcements are provided to alert passengers of the lift car’s arrival and its direction of travel. These features significantly enhance safety and accessibility by ensuring that all users, including those with visual impairments, can use the lift confidently and independently.

Electrical Safety with Residual Current Circuit Breakers

Electric shock is a potential hazard during lift operation and maintenance activities. To protect residents, users, and maintenance personnel, a safe power supply system is installed for the lift, incorporating residual current circuit breakers (RCCBs) with overload protection. These safety devices are applied to the lighting power circuits within the lift car interior, lift shaft, and at both the top and bottom of the lift car, effectively reducing the risk of electric shock and enhancing overall safety.

Anti-Hand Trapping Devices on Lift Doors

An anti-hand trapping device is installed on the lift doors to automatically stop their operation if a passenger’s hand is detected in the door gap, preventing injury and enhancing user safety.

Infrared Non-Contact Lift Door Detection

A non-contact detection system using multiple infrared beams is installed near the lift doors to sense the presence of objects or individuals. This system enhances safety by preventing the doors from closing whenever an obstruction is detected, thereby reducing the risk of injury to residents and users.

Escalator Safety with Warning Stripes

Accidents caused by slips, trips, or falls can occur while using escalators. To help prevent these incidents, a 150mm-wide yellow warning stripe is painted along the three edges of each step and the top edge of the apron. This visual alert enhances awareness for passengers and maintenance workers, promoting safer use of the escalator.

Safety inside Plant Rooms

To provide safe working environment for the maintenance workers, all mechanical moving parts of the machine inside plant rooms should be effectively guarded to avoid bodily contact and prevent injury. Insulated rubber mats are also recommended to be placed adjacent to electrical panels/switches for better protection against electric shock during maintenance work. For objects such as plinth, kerb, floor trunking etc. protruding from the floor/corners which may possess tripping hazards, the objects should be painted with clear and prominent colour (such as yellow and black stripes) as warning signs to alert the workers.

Volume Control Devices for Refuse Chutes

Refuse Chute is provided for each domestic block with waste collected in refuse bins located in the ground floor refuse collection chamber. However, workers may be injured due to hazards arising from overfilled refuse bins. To address this safety concern, a Volume Control Storage Device has been incorporated into the refuse chute system to regulate the volume of refuse, preventing overfilling and reducing the risk of injury to maintenance and refuse collection workers.

Bin Cleansing Machine for Safe Waste Handling

The refuse bins used have a capacity of 660 litres, with dimensions approximately 1370 mm long × 775 mm wide × 1185 mm high, and weigh around 35kg (net weight when empty). These bins require regular cleaning and maintenance to ensure hygiene and safety. Traditionally, workers manually maneuver the bins during washing, which demands significant physical effort and increases the risk of strain or injury. To reduce the physical burden on workers and enhance safety, a Bin Cleaning Machine has been introduced. This machine automatically lifts the bins, making it easier and safer for workers to clean the interior without excessive manual handling.
