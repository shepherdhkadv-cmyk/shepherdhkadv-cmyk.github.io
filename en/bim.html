Application of BIM and Innovative Technology

Building Information Modelling (BIM) technology allows professionals in the construction industry to carry out planning, design and construction works in a virtual environment, which helps to reduce risks, avoid changes and render clarity in project delivery. BIM has been applied and other innovative technologies to enhance safety in public housing development by enabling detailed visualisation and simulation of building processes, allowing for better planning, risk assessment, and coordination among stakeholders to identify and mitigate potential hazards before they occur. We have proactively applied these innovative technologies through the project life cycle from planning, design and construction in our projects.


BIM and other innovative technologies provides a platform for the safety management to:
Examine the project design
Examine construction programme
Examine working space
Examine the relationship between design, program and space
Conduct safety training instead of real life mock-up

BIM and other innovative technologies could eventually:
Reduce construction risk
Reduce construction accidents
Reduce construction time
Reduce construction cost

Detailed Design

Generative Design

Generative Design is a progressive design process that uses computational techniques and artificial intelligence to automate the generation of feasible design options based on specific goals and parameters, which can enhance the analysis and decision-making process. This methodology is applied in the HA-developed “BIM-enabled Systematic Approach to Foundation Design” (BIM-SAFD).


Foundation design (BIM-SAFD)

BIM-SAFD is an innovative foundation design approach that provides a comprehensive automatic generation solution for foundation design. It enables visualisation of the spatial arrangement of pilings and drill-holes, which can help improve the construction safety by identifying hazards before they become problems, and avoid physical risks by visualising and planning site logistics ahead of time.

3D Coordination

Transplanting feature trees during the planning and construction stages is often challenging and presents significant safety risks due to the complexity of the work and site constraints related to transportation, lifting operations, and tree condition. The integration of 3D laser scanning (point cloud), Mobile Mapping Systems (MMS), and BIM streamlines the transplantation process by providing spatial data for analyzing the optimal orientation of trees. This technology reduces reliance on laborers and minimizes the risk of personal injury.

BIM for Construction / Safety Planning and Risk Mitigation

Design conflict detection between the structural frame (in green) with the cladding walls

In recent years BIM is used to conduct extensive pre-construction studies and verification to ascertain the buildability of our designs, minimise risks and enhance construction and safety planning so that problems can be solved and risks can be mitigated before actual work is carried out on site. As a start, we focus on tender stage which has proven to bring significant benefits.
In the past, contractors submitted traditional 2D plans and bar chart schedules as part of the technical proposal for complex building and foundation tenders, which were difficult to visualise. Starting from 2020, contractors are requested to submit simulation (4D modelling) videos that illustrate the construction process, demonstrate construction planning, and identify potential risks. With the simulation videos, the construction works and sequence can be clearly demonstrated, allowing project teams to assess whether the contractor has an accurate understanding of the work involved and if their construction program is feasible. The benefits are especially significant in complex projects with congested sites.

Construction Planning

Simulation videos were prepared by the building contractors to present the site layout planning, including details of tower crane, logistic delivery, temporary works and storage. They covered the operations of the entire site, allowing the contractor to better demonstrate the feasibility of the project and control the limited time for the construction of the project.

This foundation project involved diversion of public walkway, construction of large diameter bored pile, socketed steel-H pile, extensive and staged excavation, lateral support works and pile cap works. Simulation videos were useful for the tenderers to demonstrate the feasibility of their proposed solutions on work sequence and safety planning, especially as some works are concurrent.

Safety

The site for this project is very congested and in close proximity to existing domestic blocks and MTRC light rail. To prevent obstruction of the ground floor during the construction process, a temporary tunnel with portal frame will be erected to maintain the vehicle access during the construction of mezzanine floor and transfer plate of a domestic block. To prevent object falling to the light rail, protective canopies will be erected before commencement of 1/F. The timing and the extent of these safety measures planned by the contractor were demonstrated clearly in the video.

Risk Mitigation

The simulation video demonstrates that the contractor for this project decided to excavate and build the basement carpark as early as possible to minimise the risk of falling objects during frequent lifting operation during the construction of typical floors.
By reviewing the height of cranes and the adjacent buildings and slopes in the simulation video rather than just reviewing on a 2D layout plan, luffing cranes were selected to minimise the risk of collision between the cranes and the surrounding buildings and slopes.

Assist Tender Assessment

The simulation video is an efficient visualisation tool to assist the tender assessment on the contractors’ proposals. It demonstrates the construction works sequence in 3D and corresponds with the master programme, which helps eliminate misunderstandings. Assessment could be made easily with the simulation video on whether the proposal was logical and realistic, and whether the contractor has sufficient understanding on the site and its constraints.

Site Coordination

The simulation video enhanced early foreseeability of site constraints, construction difficulties and risks identification at the tender stage. It reduced risks due to improvement of team coordination and collaboration. The contractor could easily link the simulation video with activities of other parties and proceed planning in a logical manner by maximising the work efficiency of subcontractors.

Demonstration of Innovative Technology

Compared with the traditional 2D presentation, the simulation video is especially useful for illustrating innovative technology, like Modular Integrated Construction (MiC) in this project. The 3D models with the added dimension of time could clearly demonstrate the installation, buildability and safety of construction, including the MiC modules and components in contractor’s planning and sequencing. The simulation video could facilitate contractor to manage and track delivery and storage of MiC components/modules throughout each step of manufacturing, packing, logistics and delivery.

Development and Construction Site Mobile System (DCSMS)

This system allows the contractor and site staff to promptly report any site accident/incident using the web or the safety alert mobile app. This platform also captures the data in accident/incident reports submitted by contractors and site staff to facilitate retrieval of submission records for monitoring by related parties, allow convenient generation of statistics report, assist users to identify trends and causes of accidents and incidents, and alert users when an alert limit or a warning threshold is exceeded.

Early Fault Detection by Internet of Things (IoT) Monitoring

Continuous monitoring of temperature and humidity levels in plant rooms is crucial for maintaining optimal operating conditions. By detecting abnormal fluctuations, the system can alert operators to potential overheating, allowing for immediate intervention to prevent equipment failure and reduce the risk of fire hazards.
Early detection of water leaks is crucial for preventing electrical hazards that can endanger personnel and damage equipment. By identifying leaks promptly, the system ensures a safer working environment and minimises costly repairs.
Low-level monitoring of water tanks is essential for ensuring safety and operational efficiency. By promptly detecting low water levels, the system alerts staff to take immediate action, preventing potential hazards and minimising the risk of equipment damage due to insufficient water supply.
Door status monitoring is essential for preventing unauthorized access to plant rooms, thereby enhancing safety and ensuring that only authorised personnel can operate critical systems.

The IoT system enables real-time monitoring to enhance safety in plant rooms, facilitating early fault detection and allowing for timely preventive maintenance to mitigate risks, thereby significantly reducing the potential for accidents and ensuring a safer working environment.
